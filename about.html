Introduction

Me: social creature, food-loving, almost always remember dreams, once secured an interview by writing a haiku. When I moved to New York at the end of February 2014, it was the first time I had ever visited the city, job prospects were zero, and I had no idea I'd be living on a pullout couch for the next four months.

Important to me: Family, friends, and a category I’ll just label “art” that includes new experiences, education, and a giving-and-receiving of expression. I want to become fluent in French and Spanish (which seems pretty reasonable given that so many people I’ve met from other countries are like, “Oh yeah, I speak eight languages.”), get a grasp of history, and become a good enough writer to move people who like words. Food, again, remains one of my greatest joys in this life.

Where I'm From

I’m from a suburb of Little Rock, Arkansas called Conway.

More About Me

What are your favorite hobbies?

Have I mentioned that I love eating? Eating alone, eating with friends, eating in exotic or mundane locales. So much eating, and I enjoy it all. Talking about weird things and ideas. Drink, dance, and travel. Also: movies, music, and reading. I do a lot of writing or thinking about writing, but “favorite” isn’t quite the right word—we’ve sort of got a love-hate-compulsion thing going. Same goes for exercise.

What's your dream job?

Dream world, dream job (a hybrid): My "day job" is as a journalist, so I get paid to talk to new people and stay informed about what’s going on in the real world. In my free time, I’m an author, and I write about whatever strange subject matter I want because it’s not what pays the bills. (My professional reputation isn’t compromised at all—and is actually helped—by being a little uncouth, and I somehow have enough time to pursue both roles.) One of the things I write is scripts, with the occasional acting in my own or friends’ films. On the weekends, I go dancing and sing in small, dimly lit bars to people who aren't embarrassed for me at all. My piano repertoire has definitely expanded from fragmented YouTube tutorials and a few measures from a level-5 piano book song called "Romance" that I learned in about 1999. Actually, it’s looking like I may be...I’m...Miranda July?
